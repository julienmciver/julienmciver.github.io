Welding
Metal Active Gas (MAG) welding, Tungsten Inert Gas (TIG), Manual Metal Arc (MMA) and Submerged Arc Welding (SAW) welding equipment is available on our premises. Wherever possible, the welding is semi-automated by a wide variety of manipulators and welding booms.

We have a library of welding procedures suitable for ASME and EN ISO coded welding on most power station and pressure vessel steels. All of our weld repairs to a wide variety of metals are backed by extensive documentation (i.e. welding procedure specification, qualification and inspection). We offer heat treatment of the finished welding and semi-automatic welding of most cylindrical components. We are also able to perform weld overlay cladding of internal bores down to 25mm internal diameter.

All of our welding staff are coded to ASME and European EN ISO standards. To this end we can offer welding that has a very low rate of distortion with very tight dimensions, good surface finishes and high tolerances.

At Applied Welding Technology, we are able to weld a diverse variety of metals:

  • Steels, such as basic carbon steels and stainless steels
  • x20CrMoV121;
  • Nickel alloys, such as Inconel
  • Tin and Aluminium Bronze;
  • Aluminium and Cast iron.

Machining
